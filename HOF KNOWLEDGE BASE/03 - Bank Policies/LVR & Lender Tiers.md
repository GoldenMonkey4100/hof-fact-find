# LVR & Lender Tiers

## LVR Thresholds — General Rules

| LVR | Implication |
|---|---|
| ≤ 60% | Best pricing tier at most lenders; some specialist products available |
| ≤ 70% | Strong position; Low Doc typically available |
| ≤ 80% | Standard lending without LMI; maximum for most Low Doc / Alt Doc |
| 80–85% | LMI required (most lenders); some lenders allow 85% without LMI (professionals) |
| 85–90% | LMI required; lender appetite varies significantly |
| 90–95% | LMI required; limited lender appetite; FHLDS/FHBG schemes can waive LMI at 95% |
| > 95% | Effectively only available via government guarantee schemes |

**LMI (Lender's Mortgage Insurance):** Protects the lender (not borrower) against default. Premium paid once at settlement (can be capitalised into loan). Premium tables vary by lender and insurer (QBE vs Arch).

## Lender Tier Framework

### Tier 1 — Major Banks (CBA, ANZ, NAB, Westpac + subsidiaries)
- Lowest rates; most conservative policy
- Strong serviceability calculators (high floor rates, ~3% buffer)
- Prefer PAYG, 2+ years employment, 20% genuine savings (or 5% + LMI)
- Typically require 3 months genuine savings evidence

### Tier 2 — Second-Tier Banks (Macquarie, Bankwest, ING, Suncorp, BOQ, ME Bank, etc.)
- Competitive rates; slightly more flexible policy
- Some have niche strengths (e.g. Macquarie for investors, ING for offset)
- Generally similar servicing requirements to Tier 1

### Tier 3 — Non-Bank Lenders (Pepper, La Trobe, Liberty, Bluestone, RedZed, etc.)
- Higher rates; most flexible policy
- Credit impairment tolerated (defaults, judgments — see lender matrix)
- Alternative income verification (BAS, bank statements, accountant letter)
- Useful for self-employed, casual workers, non-residents, credit-impaired borrowers

### Specialist / Private Lenders
- Short-term bridging; very high rates
- Asset-backed lending (LVR-focused, minimal income verification)
- Use only when mainstream options exhausted

## Common Policy Points

### Genuine Savings
Most Tier 1/2 lenders require **5% of purchase price** in genuine savings held for 3+ months when LVR > 85%. Acceptable sources:
- Bank/savings account (consistent balance or regular deposits)
- Term deposits
- Shares held 3+ months
- Equity in existing property
- First Home Super Saver (FHSS) releases

**Not genuine savings (typically):** Gift funds (unless held 3+ months), proceeds from asset sale (lender specific), inheritance.

### Employment Conditions
- **New job:** Many lenders require probation to be complete (or accept a letter confirming permanent after probation)
- **Changed industry:** Some lenders require 6–12 months in new industry
- **Casual → Permanent:** Date of permanent confirmation matters

### Self-Employed Minimum Periods
- **2 years ABN** is the near-universal minimum for standard Full Doc
- **< 2 years ABN:** Non-bank lenders may accept with strong industry experience + prior PAYG tax return in same field

### Application Types

| Type | Income Verification | LVR Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Full Doc | Full payslips / tax returns / NOAs | Up to 95% |
| Low Doc / Alt Doc | BAS + accountant declaration OR 6–12 months bank statements | Typically 80% max |
| SMSF | SMSF financial statements + deed + rental income | Typically 70–80% max |

## Serviceability Buffer

APRA requires lenders to assess borrowers at **floor rate of minimum 3% above loan rate** (or 5.5% floor, whichever is higher). This is the primary lever that determines borrowing capacity.

Some non-bank lenders (not APRA-regulated) apply lower buffers — note this when recommending.

## Cross-Collateralisation

- Linking multiple securities under one lender
- **Risk:** Makes it harder to sell one property without lender consent; may limit future refinancing flexibility
- **Benefit:** Can consolidate borrowing, may improve LVR position
- HOF fact find captures `crossCollateralise` flag per security — note this in Notion for processing team
